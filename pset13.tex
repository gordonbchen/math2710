\question  Explain the precise definition of a limit in your own words. What is the role of $\delta$ and of $\varepsilon$?

\begin{solution}\\
$\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \iff  \forall \varepsilon > 0, \exists \delta > 0, \forall x:
0 < |x - a| < \delta \implies |f(x) - L| < \varepsilon$.

$\delta$ is how far $x$ can be from $a$ so that $f(x)$ is at most $\varepsilon$ away from $L$.
\end{solution}

\question Prove, using the $\delta, \epsilon$ defintion of limits, that: $\lim_{x \to 3} 4x+6=18.$

\begin{solution}

WTS:

$\forall \varepsilon > 0, \exists \delta > 0, \forall x: 0 < |x - 3| < \delta \implies |4x+6 - 18| < \varepsilon$.
\begin{align*}
    |4x+6-18| &< \varepsilon \\
    |4x-12| &< \varepsilon \\
    |x-3| &< \frac{\varepsilon}{4}
\end{align*}

So choose $\delta = \frac{\varepsilon}{4}$.

\begin{proof}
    Let $\varepsilon > 0$. Let $\delta = \frac{\varepsilon}{4}$.

    Assume that $|x-3| < \delta$.
    \begin{align*}
        |x-3| < \delta \\
        |x-3| < \frac{\varepsilon}{4} \\
        |4x-12| < \varepsilon \\
        |4x+6 - 18| < \varepsilon \\
    \end{align*}

    Thus we conclude that $\lim_{x \to 3} 4x + 6 = 18$.
\end{proof}\end{solution}
